Below is the output for one test run with the naive simulation policy and the
dumb learner. One road had a 70% saturation of cars while the other had
a 20% saturation of cars. The average queue lengths are the total number of cars
queued at either intersection at each cycle, averaged across sets of 25
consecutive cycles.

The results show the queue length initially increasing and then remaning
slightly below 50. This is because the dumb learner has a static light change
time and is not able to adjust to the higher number of cars on one road. Hence,
the busier road is remains full or almost full.
